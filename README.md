# pdb-homology-leakage-benchmark

Code, data, and results for the paper:

**Homology Leakage Inflates Reported Performance in PDB Protein Functional-Class Classification: A Sequence-Identity Re-Benchmark with a Memorisation Probe and a Frozen Protein Language Model**
Afaque Alam and Mukesh Kumar, Department of Computer Science and Engineering, National Institute of Technology Patna, India.

---

## Overview

Protein functional-class classification from sequence is a common benchmark task, and the RCSB Protein Data Bank "Structural Protein Sequences" collection distributed through Kaggle is one of its most popular datasets. Almost all studies on it use a random train/test split. Because the dataset is dominated by families of near-identical sequences, a random split places homologous chains on both sides of the partition, so a model can score highly by retrieving a near-duplicate rather than by learning function.

This repository reproduces our re-benchmark of that dataset under a leakage-free protocol. It curates a label-audited subset of 40,211 unique sequences across ten functional classes, clusters them with MMseqs2 at 30% sequence identity, and compares a conventional random split against a cluster split that keeps whole clusters intact. It evaluates four models over ten seeds: a 1-nearest-neighbour memorisation probe, logistic regression and a linear SVM on 3-mer TF-IDF features, and a frozen ESM-2 (650M) mean-pooled linear probe. It also measures leakage directly and sweeps the clustering identity from 90% down to 20%.

## Key results

Macro-F1 (mean over 10 seeds), random split vs leakage-free 30%-identity cluster split:

| Model | Random | Cluster | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-NN (memorisation probe) | 0.770 | 0.259 | −66% |
| Logistic regression (k-mer) | 0.602 | 0.222 | −63% |
| Linear SVM (k-mer) | 0.666 | 0.247 | −63% |
| ESM-2 650M frozen + probe | 0.797 | **0.576** | **−28%** |

- Independently of any model, **86.5% of random-split test sequences already have a ≥30%-identity homolog in the training set.**
- A one-nearest-neighbour probe that can only retrieve the most similar training sequence reaches **0.77 macro-F1** under the random split, showing the task is largely a homology-lookup problem.
- Across clustering identities from 90% to 20%, the effect is monotonic and the ESM-2 advantage over the strongest baseline **widens** from about 0.10 to 0.32 macro-F1 as homology is removed.

## Requirements

- Python 3.10+
- MMseqs2 (release 15 or later)
- Python packages: `numpy`, `pandas`, `scikit-learn`, `scipy`, `matplotlib`, `torch`, `transformers`, `fair-esm`
- A GPU is recommended for computing the ESM-2 embeddings (Section 4); everything else runs on CPU.

Pinned versions are in `environment.yml`. The notebook was developed and run on Google Colab with a mounted Google Drive.

## How to reproduce

The pipeline is a single Colab notebook, `notebooks/PDB_leakage_rebenchmark_10seed_drive.ipynb`, organised into numbered sections that run top to bottom. Each stage checkpoints to disk and is resumable, so an interrupted run continues from the last completed step.

1. **Get the source data.** Download the two files from the Kaggle dataset `shahir/protein-data-set` (`pdb_data_seq.csv` and `pdb_data_no_dups.csv`) and place them where the notebook expects (Section 1).
2. **Section 2 — Build the dataset.** Merges the files, takes labels from `pdb_data_no_dups.csv` only, keeps canonical-residue protein chains of length 50–1000, removes exact duplicates and conflicting-label sequences, and keeps the ten most frequent classes. Prints the counts, including the number of conflicting-label sequences removed.
3. **Section 3 — Cluster at 30% identity.** Runs MMseqs2 `easy-cluster` (`--min-seq-id 0.3 -c 0.8 --cov-mode 1`) and writes `dataset_clustered.csv`.
4. **Section 4 — ESM-2 embeddings.** Mean-pools the final layer of `esm2_t33_650M_UR50D` per sequence and caches the result (checkpointed).
5. **Section 5 — Ten-seed ablation.** For each seed, builds both splits and evaluates all four models; writes `results_10seed.csv` (row-level resume).
6. **Section 6 — Leakage exposure.** Computes, model-free, the fraction of random-split test sequences with a training homolog (reproduces the 86.5% figure).
7. **Section 6b — Threshold sweep.** Re-clusters at 90/70/50/40/30/20% identity and repeats the cluster-split evaluation; writes `sweep_results.csv` and the sweep figure.
8. **Sections 7–9 — Aggregate, figures, paired tests.** Produces the summary tables, 95% CIs, the figures, and the paired ESM-2-vs-baseline tests (with effect sizes).
9. **Section 10 — Bundle.** Copies all result files and figures into a single archive.
